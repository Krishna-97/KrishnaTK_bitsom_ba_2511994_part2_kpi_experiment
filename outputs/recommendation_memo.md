# Recommendation Memo

## Executive Summary

The A/B experiment compared the existing onboarding process (Control) with a redesigned onboarding campaign (Treatment).

The statistical analysis demonstrates that the Treatment significantly improves Paid Conversion Rate while maintaining acceptable business performance across key guardrail metrics.

---

## North Star Metric

Paid Conversion Rate

Control

22 / 693 = **3.18%**

Treatment

50 / 715 = **6.99%**

The Treatment nearly doubled the conversion rate compared with the Control group.

## Experiment Results

The Treatment group achieved a substantially higher Paid Conversion Rate than the Control group.

Hypothesis testing produced:

* P-value = 0.001146
* Alpha = 0.05

Therefore, the observed improvement is statistically significant.

---

## Guardrail Analysis

Refund requests remained low.

Support ticket activity should continue to be monitored after rollout.

Average Revenue per User across all users is approximately **52.83**.

Segment summaries indicate consistent performance across Region, Device Type, Traffic Source, and Plan Type without evidence of a major decline.

---

## Segment-Level Insights

Highest Revenue per User:

• Premium Plan (77.53)

Highest Paid Conversion Rate:

• Referral Traffic (6.98%)

Highest Regional Revenue:

• North Region (82.40)

These segments provide opportunities for future campaign optimization.

---

## Final Recommendation

**Launch the new onboarding campaign.**

Reasons:

* Paid Conversion Rate improved from **3.18%** to **6.99%**.
* Chi-Square Test confirmed the improvement is statistically significant.
* No major guardrail metric indicates unacceptable business risk.

---

## Risks and Limitations

* The experiment evaluates short-term behaviour only.
* Long-term retention and lifetime value were not measured.
* Continue monitoring support tickets and customer retention after rollout.

---

## Next Steps

1. Launch the Treatment onboarding.
2. Monitor refund and support metrics weekly.
3. Measure 60-day and 90-day retention.
4. Continue segment-level performance tracking.
